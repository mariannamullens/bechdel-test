Visualizing the Bechdel-Wallace Test
Learn more

The Bechdel Test, named after graphic novelist Alison Bechdel, measures the representation of women in film and other media. It is not a perfect measure (its shortcomings have spawned many other measures like it), but there are several data sets online that allow amateur data sleuths to analyze several decades of film using the three measures of the Bechdel test:

  • The movie has to have at least two women in it...
  • who talk to each other...
  • about something besides a man

Key

Click on decades and years to further explore movies.

  • Circle size represents domestic gross.
  • A red circle represents a movie that failed the test.
  • A white circle represents a movie that passed the test.
